# Story Idea Generator

Generate story concepts from emotional genre first, then setting/character/plot.

## Purpose

Ensure concept decisions serve reader feeling, not setting label.

## When to Use

- Starting a new story concept
- Brainstorming multiple story premises
- Strengthening weak/unclear concept
- Fixing setting-rich but emotionally vague idea

## When Not to Use

- You already have validated concept and need execution planning
- You need full drafting/prose generation workflow

## Workflow

1. **Choose emotional core**
   - select primary genre from 11 elemental genres
   - add optional secondary genre(s) that support primary
2. **Choose setting expressions**
   - keep only elements that amplify primary emotional experience
3. **Design genre-fit characters**
   - traits/vulnerabilities that let genre hit hard
4. **Build high concept**
   - concise pitch + central conflict direction
5. **Evaluate and refine**
   - genre clarity
   - setting fit
   - character fit
   - thematic resonance
   - originality

## Output Checklist

- [ ] Primary genre explicitly named by reader emotion
- [ ] Secondary genre (if any) supports primary
- [ ] Setting choices justified by genre impact
- [ ] Characters positioned for genre pressure
- [ ] 1–2 sentence concept pitch produced
- [ ] Weakest evaluation dimension improved

## Guardrails

- Don’t confuse setting with genre
- Don’t let secondary genre overtake primary
- Don’t treat requirements as checklist-only mechanics
- Don’t keep characters unaffected by genre events
